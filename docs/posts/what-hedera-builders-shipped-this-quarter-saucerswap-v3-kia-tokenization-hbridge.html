SaucerSwap’s multi‑layer execution model — specifics engineers need

SaucerSwap runs three execution layers with distinct tradeoffs and a clear hybrid strategy for V3.

  • V1 (2022): classic constant‑product AMM. LPs deposit equal amounts of two tokens; pool covers full price range. Always‑available liquidity; tolerant to extreme price moves and newly minted tokens.
  • V2: concentrated liquidity (Uniswap v3 concept). LPs pick price ranges to concentrate capital for lower slippage, single‑sided exposure. Introduces programmatic incentives:
  • "Larry" (Liquidity Aligned Rewards): two‑week epochs favoring concentrated LPs and market commitments.
  • Bonzai/Bonzo vaults: automation primitives that detect out‑of‑range positions and reallocate capital automatically—useful for integrators and market makers.
  • V3: hybrid CLOB + AMM model.
  • Users "sign an intent" (bid/ask) with no fee at sign time; an intent is executed only when filled and can be cancelled without fee.
  • Execution: match inside V3 CLOB; fall back to V1/V2 pools when orders can’t be satisfied.
  • Fees: platform fee = 0.3% per trade (≈0.25% LPs, ≈0.05% treasury). Makers pay 0 on fill; takers pay 5–15 bps.
  • Goal: provide order‑book behavior familiar to institutions while preserving on‑chain LP depth via AMM fallback.

Engineers building integrations should simulate both matching and AMM fallback paths and test maker/taker fee flows.
